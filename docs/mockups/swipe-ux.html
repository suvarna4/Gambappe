CommittedThe receipt prints upward from the bottom edge — perforation, mono numerals, entry price, timestamp. Undo stays printed on it for 60s (§10.3). The crowd split stays sealed until lock, so your call is yours P1.

1

The streak flame is chrome, not content. It never leaves the top bar, so the cost of skipping a day is visible at the exact moment you could fix it.

2

Side rails are the tutorial. Persistent 26px gutters in each side's color with the label and arrow. After 5 picks they fade to 40% — learned hands don't need signage.

3

Commit threshold ≈ 36% of card width. Below it, release snaps back with an overshoot spring — no accidental picks. Crossing it fires one light haptic tick; the card "arms."

4

The release is the timestamp. Entry price is stamped at the moment your finger leaves the glass — the atomic unit of the whole product (PRD §6.1) made physical. P4

5

First-pick 18+ gate: the throw pauses held at the threshold, attest line prints on the card's edge — "I'm 18 or older → confirm". Confirm completes the throw; cancel springs back. The gate lives inside the gesture, not in a modal over it.

6

Tomorrow is already under today. The under-card peeks from the deck, so finishing today physically reveals the appointment for tomorrow P8.

7

Undo is printed, not floated. A quiet mono link on the receipt itself, counting down 60s. Undo retracts the receipt back into the slot and returns the card to your hand.

The loser's artifactBusted streaks get a designed obituary card — dates, cause of death, what it survived. Equal or greater craft than the win card, because the loss screenshot is the funnier one P3.

1

Losing publicly, with style, is the brand. The obituary is written by the app from data (P1): no user performs grief. "Bury it" archives it to your profile's graveyard shelf; sharing it is the strongest recruitment card we mint.

2

The freeze is a save, not a purchase. One earned per week by showing up. When a freeze auto-spends, the flame frosts over for the day — visible, slightly embarrassing, never monetized.

3

Tomorrow is the consolation. The next card literally peeks from under the obituary. The comeback arc starts nine hours from now, and the copy says so: "Streak 0. Everyone's is, eventually."

The crowd

One room with 30,000 people,
one room with two

Community here is concentric, and every ring is entered by the same gesture. You never "join" anything — you throw a card, and the structures catch you P5. The big room supplies scale and spectacle; the small rooms supply names, grudges, and chemistry. Both are outputs of matchmaking, not prerequisites.

You

A streak, a fingerprint, a public record page. Value accrues before identity — the ghost earns it, signup just claims it.

Your duo

One stranger, chemistry stat, shared receipts. The smallest possible team; consent renewed weekly by a throw.

Your nemesis

One rival, a narrated week, lifetime lore. Drama engineered from style distance, never skill gaps P12.

The name

Neither the meme nor the memo

"Gambappe" tells strangers we're a joke before they've seen the product; "Receipts" tells them we're a compliance department — and "prove your prowess" is a solo brag in a product whose whole soul is losing together on purpose. The bar: a plain word you'd say out loud anyway P11, internet-native without an expiry date, zero money-words (the legal posture is also the brand), and mouthfeel that survives being yelled across a bar. Candidates, ranked:

RECOMMENDED
CALLED IT
calledit.app · @calledit

The phrase you already shout at the TV. It names the exact second the product exists for — the reveal — and it's what winners and losers say ("called it" / "you called it, fine"). The best artifact in the product (the ≤20¢ longshot badge) already bears this name; the brand is the badge. Verb-first app names have strong precedent (Letterboxd, Untappd).

RISK Two words to type; check collisions with generic "calledit" handles. Slight win-side lean — mitigated because the obituary card answers it ("didn't call it").

RUNNER-UP
SIDES
sides.app · pick your side

The name is the gesture. Left side, right side; taking sides; "whose side are you on?" — it names the swipe, the crowd split, the nemesis, and the houses with one household word. Warmest communal framing of the bunch: a side is something you're on, with other people.

RISK Generic enough that SEO and trademark need care; mild "fries with that" joke surface. Needs the two-color wordmark to feel owned.

HEAT CHECK
heatcheck.app · daily at 9

Basketball slang for testing a hot streak — which is literally the streak mechanic. "Today's heat check" names the daily ritual for free, and it's confident without being braggy (a heat check is allowed to miss; that's the fun).

RISK Sports-slang lean may read narrow for politics/econ questions; two words; US-centric.

clocked.
clocked.app · you clocked it early

"Clocked it" = saw it coming — and every pick in the product is literally clocked (timestamped, price-stamped at release). The double meaning is the whole architecture in one word. Reads tasteful-internet, not meme-internet.

RISK Subtle — needs one tagline to teach it. UK/AAVE slang origin; verify it reads globally.

LORE†
lore.app · your rivalry has lore now

What the product manufactures is lore: narrated rivalry weeks, season archives, graveyard shelves, "she owns your Tuesdays." Internet culture's fondest word for exactly this. Community-first by definition — lore only exists between people.

RISK Says nothing about prediction; premium domain likely brutal; several small products already named Lore.

JINX
jinx.app · don't jinx it

The superstition layer of prediction culture — "don't jinx it" is what a streak makes everyone say, and "jinx!" is what two people shout when they match (hello, duos). Short, playful, game-shaped.

RISK Heavy collisions (Riot's Jinx, merch brands). Leans cute; the receipts-credibility story gets no help from it.

Honorable mentions: Gut Check (warmer Heat Check, less streak-native), Longshot (great energy, one-sided), Scrim (esports-native, too niche outside gaming), Rec (rec-league dignity + "record," needs a tagline to land). Process note: the repo can stay gambappe; the product name is one constant (PRODUCT_NAME in packages/core) plus domains — rename is a one-day change, so run the trademark/domain screen on the top two before Question Zero, per the PRD's open question.
