The mechanism

How a comment becomes a number

weight = log1p(upvotes)α · homerDiscount · ½(age / halfLife)   — every knob lives in the skill, not the code

A deterministic extractor reads each comment sentence by sentence: team aliases (including “homecoming” and “south beach”), signed stance cues, negation windows, sarcasm markers. Every output is reproducible and auditable — here are three real comments from the 8,880-upvote thread about the Miami Heat's accidentally-published “LeBron James Introductory Press Conference” video:

LeBron uses the Warriors as leverage. Miami tanks LeBron's value by leaking that they've already got him.

no team, no stance → weight 0

Most comments emit nothing, and that's correct — dropping noise cleanly matters as much as scoring the signal.

The honest card

What this does and doesn't prove

The exam is sealed by physics, not policy. Every prior test of a model risks leakage. This one can't leak: the answer doesn't exist yet. On resolution we publish Brier(crowd) next to Brier(market-close), whatever they say.

Capture-once, by decision. Each live thread is snapshotted exactly once, two hours after posting — no re-crawling, no survivor-editing. The fetchedAt stamp is an audit mark, not a version axis.

xTrace is load-bearing for the lifecycle, not the math. The extraction is deterministic code; what xTrace holds is the part that changes — the knobs, their history, and proof of what each version had seen. Improvement without redeploys, lineage without trust.

The crowd will be wrong sometimes. It ranked Kawhi's destination third. So did nearly everyone with money on the line. The claim isn't omniscience — it's a measured, auditable read of what the loudest room on the internet believes, priced against what the money believes.
